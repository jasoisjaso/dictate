# Dictate — YouTube Video Guide (English + Bosnian)

---

## WILL THIS DO OKAY?

**English video: YES, strong potential.** The free/offline/Whisper angle is hot in 2025-2026. Wispr Flow charges $15/mo, Superwhisper $249 lifetime — you're offering the same core experience for $0. The Wi-Fi-off moment is your shareable clip. The per-app profiles + voice commands put you ahead of every other FREE tool. Competition: whisper-local (drajb) is your closest free competitor but has no UI, no visualizer, no per-app profiles. You win on polish.

**Bosnian video: YES, and arguably BIGGER opportunity.** There are ZERO good free Bosnian dictation tools. Bosnian speakers are completely underserved — Google's dictation barely works for Bosnian, Microsoft's Win+H doesn't support it well, and no paid tool specifically targets Bosnian. A video titled "Besplatno diktiranje na bosanskom jeziku — bez interneta" has almost no competition on YouTube. The Bosnian diaspora (Australia, Europe, USA) will share this heavily. This could be the video that puts the app on the map.

**Key advice for both:** Keep it under 12 minutes. Show, don't tell. The Wi-Fi-off moment must happen in the first 60 seconds. Use good audio — you're selling a dictation tool, bad mic audio kills credibility instantly.

## ENGLISH VIDEO SCRIPT

**Title options (pick one for the thumbnail):**
- "I Made a FREE Dictation App That Never Touches the Internet"
- "Free Whisper Dictation — Better Than Wispr Flow ($0 vs $15/mo)"

**Tone:** Authentic, casual, dad-from-Brisbane. Not a tech YouTuber. Show real workflows.

### [0:00 - 0:20] HOOK — The Wi-Fi-Off Moment

**On screen:** Open Notepad. Hold Right Ctrl, dictate a sentence. While still recording, physically disable Wi-Fi (click the network icon, turn off Wi-Fi). Keep dictating. Text still appears perfectly.

**Say:** "My internet is completely off right now. No Wi-Fi, no Ethernet, nothing. And I'm still dictating flawlessly — no cloud, no subscription, no account. This is Dictate, a free app I built that runs OpenAI's Whisper entirely on your own PC."

**Tech tip:** Practice the Wi-Fi toggle so it's smooth. Have the network icon pinned to the taskbar for one-click access.

---

### [0:20 - 1:00] THE PROBLEM

**On screen:** Quick flash of Wispr Flow pricing ($15/mo), Superwhisper pricing ($249 lifetime), Dragon pricing ($300-500). Then show Windows Win+H failing or producing bad results.

**Say:** "Wispr Flow charges fifteen bucks a month. Superwhisper wants two hundred and fifty dollars for a lifetime license. Dragon is three hundred to five hundred and hasn't been updated in years. And Windows' built-in Win+H? It needs the internet, can't learn your words, and mangles punctuation. I wanted something free, private, and actually good — so I built one."

### [1:00 - 2:00] INSTALL & FIRST RUN

**On screen:** Screen-record the install process. Show the SmartScreen warning ("More info" -> "Run anyway"). Show the first-run wizard: language picker, mic picker, model download progress bar. Show the "warming up" log line. Green tray icon appears.

**Say:** "Install is dead simple — download the installer, run it. Windows will show a scary blue warning because I'm not a big company with a code-signing certificate. Click 'More info', 'Run anyway'. It installs to your user folder — no admin password needed. First launch downloads the speech model once — about 500 MB. After that, it works offline forever. See that? 'Model warmed up.' The first time you press talk, it's instant — no loading delay."

**Tech tip:** Pre-download the model before recording so the progress bar is instant. Or record the download in real-time and speed it up in editing.

### [2:00 - 3:30] PUSH-TO-TALK + SPOKEN PUNCTUATION + FILLER REMOVAL

**On screen:** Open Notepad. Hold Right Ctrl. Speak naturally with punctuation commands. Show the overlay (equalizer or blob). Show text appearing.

**Say:** "Okay, the main event. I hold Right Ctrl — see the overlay pop up? Those bars are reacting to my voice in real time. I just talk:

'Hey team comma new line just confirming the pallet pickup for tomorrow period new line the hand roll towels are running low comma we need another box period new line cheers comma Dave period'

Let go. Boom — clean text with commas, periods, new lines. No 'um' or 'uh' — those get stripped automatically. You can say 'question mark', 'exclamation mark', 'semicolon', 'colon', 'bullet point', 'new paragraph' — it knows them all."

**Tech tip:** Speak at a natural pace. Don't rush. Show the text appearing after you release the key — the speed of that is the 'wow' moment.

### [3:30 - 4:30] DICTATION MODES + PER-APP PROFILES

**On screen:** Press F7. Toast says "Mode: Prose". Press again: "Mode: Code". Press again: "Mode: Email". Then open three windows: a terminal (PowerShell), Discord, and an email draft. Dictate the same sentence into each.

**Say:** "Press F7 to cycle modes: Auto, Prose, Code, Email. But the cool part is — in Auto mode, it detects which app you're in and adapts automatically. Watch: I dictate into a terminal — verbatim, no capitalisation, no cleanup. Same sentence into Discord — casual. Into an email — professional tone. Same words, different output, automatically. This is the 'Super Mode' Superwhisper charges two hundred and fifty dollars for. Free, on Windows, offline."

**Tech tip:** Have the three windows pre-opened behind Notepad. Alt-Tab between them smoothly.

### [4:30 - 6:00] VOICE COMMANDS — THE "IT'S A REAL TOOL" SEGMENT

**On screen:** Dictate a paragraph into WordPad. Then demonstrate each voice command one at a time:

1. "scratch that" — paragraph deleted
2. Dictate again. "delete last word" — last word gone
3. "delete last three words" — three words gone
4. "delete last sentence" — sentence gone
5. "capitalize that" — re-injected with Title Case
6. "all caps that" — re-injected UPPERCASE
7. "bold that" — wraps in **markdown bold**
8. "italic that" — wraps in *italic*
9. "replace hello with hi" — find-and-replace
10. "redo verbatim" — re-injects raw words without cleanup
11. "undo that" — alias for scratch

**Say:** "This is what separates a toy from a tool. I can fix mistakes by voice — no keyboard needed. 'Scratch that' deletes the whole last bit. 'Delete last word' removes just the last word. 'Delete last sentence' wipes from the last period. 'Capitalize that', 'all caps that', 'lowercase that'. 'Bold that' wraps it in markdown. And my favourite — 'replace hello with hi' — it finds the word and swaps it. If the cleanup was too aggressive, 'redo verbatim' puts the raw words back. All by voice."

**Tech tip:** Practice the sequence. Each command should be a separate dictation (hold Ctrl, say command, release). The toast confirms each action.

### [6:00 - 6:30] COPY LAST + RE-RECORD + PAUSE

**On screen:** Press F8 — toast "copied last dictation". Paste into a different window with Ctrl+V. Then press F6 — last text deleted, recording starts immediately. Then press Pause key while recording — toast "paused". Press again — "resumed".

**Say:** "Three rescue buttons. F8 copies your last dictation to the clipboard — for when text landed in the wrong window. F6 deletes the last dictation and instantly starts re-recording — one button, no scratch-then-talk dance. And the Pause key pauses mid-recording — someone walks in, you pause, they leave, you resume. Nothing lost."

### [2:00 - 3:30] DIKTIRANJE SA INTERPUNKCIJOM

## PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

### Before recording:
- [ ] Close all other apps (browser, Discord, etc.) — they can steal focus during dictation
- [ ] Test the mic with the app's mic test button
- [ ] Pre-download the Whisper model so first run is instant
- [ ] Have Notepad, WordPad, a terminal, Discord, and an email draft pre-opened
- [ ] Pin the Wi-Fi toggle to the taskbar for the hook shot
- [ ] Set the visualizer to your preferred style (blob or equalizer)
- [ ] Practice the voice command sequence 3 times — it needs to flow
- [ ] Set language to English (English video) or Bosnian (Bosnian video)

### Audio:
- [ ] Use a decent mic — NOT the laptop built-in. You're selling dictation; bad audio = no credibility
- [ ] Record in a quiet room. Background noise hurts Whisper accuracy AND viewer experience
- [ ] Do a 10-second test recording and play it back before starting

### Editing:
- [ ] Cut dead air between dictations (the silence while holding the key before speaking)
- [ ] Add the Wi-Fi-off moment as a 5-second teaser at the very start before the intro
- [ ] Zoom in on the toast messages (word count, mode changes, command confirmations) — they're small on a 1080p recording
- [ ] Add chapter timestamps in the description
- [ ] Thumbnail: split screen — crossed-out "$15/mo" on one side, "FREE · OFFLINE" + the app on the other

### For the Bosnian video specifically:
- [ ] Title in Bosnian: "Besplatno diktiranje na bosanskom — bez interneta"
- [ ] Description in both Bosnian and English (for diaspora who search in English)
- [ ] Tags: bosanski jezik, diktiranje, whisper, diktafon, govor u tekst, speech to text bosnian
- [ ] The Wi-Fi-off moment is even more powerful here — emphasize "niko ne čuje vaš glas"
